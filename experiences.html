Participants would orbit around the Earth once. During this time, the space shuttle would be orbiting around Earth, providing participants chances to view Earth from the outside. Crew members would point out space phenomena and geology cites while orbiting.

Participants would live in the International Space Station for 10 days. Guided by our crew members, passengers would experience weightlessness, conduct small experiences, and space walk outside the station.

Travelling to and from the moon takes altogether 6 days. Participants would live in the space shuttle for the duration and once on the moon, be able to walk on it, conduct small experiments, and collect moon souvenirs. A tour of the moon would be provided.

Ideally, travelling to Mars takes altogether 14 months. Participants would spend 3 months on the Red Planet touring it, and helping scientists conduct experiments.
